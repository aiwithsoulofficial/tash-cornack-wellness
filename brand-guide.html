01 / Brand Essence

The Brand

Tash Cornack is where raw, real wellness meets urban edge. A mum, a wellness advocate, and a woman who refuses to be beige. She brings holistic living into the homes of busy families - no fluff, no filtered perfection, just bold moves and genuine care.

Her brand lives at the intersection of nature and neon - boardwalk sunsets and city grit. She's the friend who tells you the truth, laughs too loud, and then hands you something that actually changes your life.

04 / Brand Voice

How Tash Sounds

Bold, Not Bossy

She speaks with confidence but never talks down. She's the friend who says "babe, you need to hear this" - direct, warm, and always coming from a place of care. No corporate speak. No wishy-washy disclaimers.

Real, Not Raw

Tash shares the truth - including the messy bits - but she's not performing struggle. She's honest about perimenopause, about parenting neurodivergent kids, about building something from scratch. Authentic without being oversharing.

Cheeky, Not Crude

"Be a badass with a good ass" energy. She can drop a one-liner that makes you snort-laugh, then pivot into something that genuinely helps. Humour is her superpower - it makes wellness feel accessible, not preachy.

Guide, Not Guru

She's not on a pedestal - she's walking beside you. She shares what worked for her family, invites you to try it, and respects your decision either way. Education over manipulation. Always.

06 / Brand Do's & Don'ts

The Rules

Yes, Always

  • Lead with HER story, not the product
  • Use casual, conversational language
  • Show the real life - messy desk, loud laugh, sunset walks
  • Educate first, sell second
  • Mix urban edge with natural warmth
  • Use bold colour - teal and pink are your anchors
  • Celebrate the body, the journey, the imperfection
  • Speak to mums, wellness seekers, and health professionals as equals

Never, Ever

  • Use scripted MLM/distributor language ("join my team!", "boss babe")
  • Make health claims about products (no "cures", "heals", "alkaline miracle")
  • Use stock photos - Tash IS the brand
  • Go pastel, minimal, or corporate
  • Put the Enagic logo bigger than her name
  • Use beige, baby pink, or washed-out tones
  • Post product-only content without her face or story
  • Sound like every other wellness page on Instagram
07 / Aesthetic Direction

The Mood

Nature meets neon. Boardwalk sunsets and city walls. The brand aesthetic lives in the tension between earthy warmth and urban boldness - a coastal sunset filtered through a neon-lit laneway. It's feminine but never fragile. Colourful but never chaotic. Think: tattoo parlour meets wellness retreat.

08 / Photo Direction

Photography Style

Selfie Queen

Tash's brand photos are real selfies - not polished studio shoots. Keep it genuine. Natural light, real expressions (the big laugh, the knowing smile), real environments. Her coloured hair and bold lip ARE the brand.

Environment Matters

Shoot in places that tell the story: home office with the laptop glowing, coastal walks, the kitchen where she makes Kangen water demos happen. Mix indoor (urban, moody) with outdoor (nature, golden hour).

Colour Pops

Her teal hair, berry lips, and colourful wardrobe ARE the palette in action. Let the brand colours show up naturally through what she wears and where she shoots. No need for heavy colour grading.

No Stock, No Flats

Never use stock photography. Never do flat-lay product shots without Tash in them. She IS the brand. Every image should feel like it was taken by a friend who caught a real moment.
